What is Milonga?

Milonga, the cradle of Tango, is a dance style characterized by vibrancy and rhythm. Milonga originated in the 19th century in Buenos Aires and Montevideo.

Today a new generation of people are taking up the Milonga dance. And for good reason: it’s a lot of fun!

How does the app work?

Your phone is your teacher - and your dance partner!

The app shows you both leader and follower roles. Just follow the visual lessons at your own pace, so your progress is stress-free

There are 36 lessons in 3 levels, using 252 original videos.

It’s suitable for both beginners and advanced dancers. The approach is totally visual. There’s no reading, no language needed!

All the steps are shown in high-quality point-of-view videos, and marked by unique icons, so they’re easy to find.

3 Meanings of the word Milonga

Milonga is a style of DANCE, as well as a distinct MUSIC genre. Its pace is faster than Tango and its steps more playful.
The term Milonga is also used for a PLACE, the salon where people meet to dance Tango and Milonga.
As an art form, Milonga blends various genres of music and movements.
After Tango took over in the end of the 19th century, Milonga made a successful comeback in the 1930s.
Today DJ's play milongas at every tango salon all over the world.
